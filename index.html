Your Life Isn't Eight Separate Projects

Most people manage their lives like a stack of unrelated tabs — career in one window, relationships in another, health somewhere you'll get to eventually. Each one gets attention only when it's loud enough to demand it. That's not a vision. That's triage.

An integrated life isn't one where every domain scores a 10. It's one where the domains actually talk to each other — where the way you work supports the way you love, and the way you rest supports both. That only happens on purpose. It doesn't happen by accident.

You don't need a better calendar. You need one vision your calendar can actually serve.

Where the Fire's Actually Burning Low

Eight domains, one ecosystem

Your life runs across eight domains: Purpose & Meaning, Relationships, Health & Vitality, Career & Impact, Financial Freedom, Personal Growth, Joy & Recreation, and Environment. None of them operate in isolation — when one is starving, the others quietly compensate, until they can't anymore.

What You Build the Vision On

Values are the non-negotiables, mission is the why

A vision built on borrowed values — what looks impressive, what your industry rewards, what your family expects — collapses the moment it gets hard. A vision built on your actual values holds, because it's not performing for anyone. It's just true.

Doesn't need to be perfect or permanent — just true right now.

If Nothing Changed

Ten years is far enough to dream, close enough to act

Without a clear vision, ten years from now just happens to you — built entirely out of whatever today's pressures and other people's expectations decided for you. With a vision, ten years from now gets built on purpose, one aligned choice at a time, starting today.

Vision Lives or Dies in the Daily Choices

A North Star you never look at isn't guiding anything

A ten-year vision means nothing if it only lives on a board you glance at twice a year. It needs a small, repeatable touchpoint — something short enough that you'll actually keep doing it past week one.
